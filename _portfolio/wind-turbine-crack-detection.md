Automating the inspection of wind turbine blades is critical for reducing operational downtime and preventing catastrophic structural failures. This project introduces a deep-learning-based crack detection and quantification framework. By deploying autonomous drones inside lab-constructed wind-turbine blade sections, we captured high-resolution surface imagery and fed it into our custom convolutional neural network pipeline. 

The system segment cracks with pixel-level precision, offering reliable measurements of crack length and width over time. Our findings and pipeline were compiled into a paper that won the **Best Paper Award** at the SPIE NDE 2026 conference.
